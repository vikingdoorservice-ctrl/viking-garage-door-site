Garage Door Spring Replacement Cost in South Carolina (2026 Guide)

By the Viking Garage Door crew · Boiling Springs, SC · July 2026

☎ Call (864) 921-7373 Free Estimate

A broken spring is the single most common garage door repair we do across Spartanburg County — and it always seems to happen on the morning you actually need the car. Here's an honest look at what the fix costs in our area in 2026 and what you're actually paying for.

The short answer: $150–$350 for most homes

For a standard residential door in the Upstate, a professional spring replacement lands between $150 and $350, parts and labor included. Where you fall in that range depends on:

  • Door size and weight — a single 8×7 door uses smaller springs than a 16×8 double, and insulated doors weigh more.
  • Spring type — torsion springs (the shaft above the door) cost a bit more than older extension springs (the stretchy ones along the tracks), but last longer and are safer.
  • One spring or two — double doors usually run two torsion springs. If one broke, the other has the same mileage; replacing both in one visit is cheaper than two service calls.
  • Cycle rating — builder-grade springs are rated ~10,000 cycles (about 7 years of normal use). High-cycle oil-tempered springs cost more upfront but last 2–4× longer. That's what we install by default.

What should be included (don't pay for less)

A proper spring job is more than swapping the coil. When we replace springs, the price includes door balancing, opener force calibration, lubrication of rollers and hinges, and a full safety inspection of the cables and bottom brackets. If a quote sounds too cheap, ask what's actually included — "spring only" jobs are how doors end up crooked a month later.

Repair vs. replace: an honest rule of thumb

If the door panels are solid and the tracks are straight, a spring replacement gives that door many more years of life — replacing the whole door because of a spring is like buying a new car because of a flat tire. But if the door is rusted through, cracked, or 25+ years old with worn hardware everywhere, it can be smarter to put that $350 toward a new door. We'll tell you straight either way.

Why you shouldn't wait (or DIY)

A door with a broken spring puts its full weight — often 150–300 lbs — on the opener and cables. Openers burn out, cables snap, and hands get hurt. If you heard the bang, stop using the door and give us a call: in most of Spartanburg, Boiling Springs, Greer and Duncan we can have it running the same day.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to replace garage door springs in South Carolina?

Most spring replacements in the Spartanburg and Greenville area run $150 to $350 installed, depending on door size, spring type and cycle rating. Oversized or custom doors can run more.

Should I replace one spring or both?

If your door has two torsion springs and one broke, replacing both usually saves money. The second spring has the same wear and typically fails soon after — and you'd pay a second service call.

Can I replace a garage door spring myself?

We don't recommend it. Torsion springs are under extreme tension and cause serious injuries every year. A professional replacement is fast and includes balancing and a safety check.
