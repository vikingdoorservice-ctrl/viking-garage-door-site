Are Insulated Garage Doors Worth It in South Carolina?

By the Viking Garage Door crew · Boiling Springs, SC · July 2026

☎ Call (864) 921-7373 Free Estimate

Around here nobody asks about garage door insulation in January — they ask in July, after walking into a garage that feels like the inside of a parked car. Here's our honest take on when the insulated upgrade is worth the money in the Upstate, and when it isn't.

What insulation actually does in a SC summer

A plain steel door is basically a metal radiator facing the sun. On a 95° afternoon, an uninsulated attached garage in Spartanburg can hit 120–130°F — and that heat leaks straight into the kitchen wall and any bonus room above. A polyurethane-insulated door (R-9 to R-13) typically keeps that same garage 10–20 degrees cooler, which your AC feels immediately.

When it's clearly worth it

  • Attached garage — the garage shares walls (and leaks heat) with living space.
  • Bonus room over the garage — the #1 "too hot in summer" room in Upstate homes.
  • You work in there — gym, workshop, laundry. 15 degrees is the difference between usable and not.
  • Bedroom near the garage — sandwich doors run dramatically quieter, no drum-skin rattle at 6 a.m.

When you can honestly skip it

Detached garage used for storage, no plans to spend time in it? Save the money — a solid non-insulated steel door protects your stuff just as well. We'd rather point that difference at better springs and rollers, which you'll notice every single day.

The numbers

On a 16×7 double door, insulation typically adds $300–$500 to the price. Our most popular door — insulated steel 16×7, installed with haul-away — runs about $1,950. Between the AC savings on an attached garage, the quieter operation and the stiffer panels (insulated doors shrug off the basketball hits that dent single-layer steel), most Upstate homeowners with attached garages come out ahead.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is an insulated garage door worth it in South Carolina?

If your garage is attached to the house, used as a workspace, or has a room above it — yes. An insulated door can keep a SC garage 10–20°F cooler in summer, cuts noise, and resists dents better. For a detached storage-only garage, it's optional.

What R-value do I need for a garage door in SC?

For most Upstate homes, R-9 to R-13 polyurethane doors hit the sweet spot of price and performance. Going higher mainly helps if the garage is climate-controlled or has living space above.

Do insulated garage doors reduce noise?

Yes, twice over: the sandwich construction rattles far less when opening, and it blocks more street noise — a real difference if there's a bedroom or office above or beside the garage.
